Section 1:Welcome

We are delighted to welcome into our home, and to the beautiful town and surroundings of Aix-les-Bains. The building, a converted former luxury hotel, is in the town’s “hypercentre”. It stands between the central square Place Carnot to its East and the town’s historical Casino Grand Cercle to its West. It is a 5-minute walk (500m) from Aix-les-Bains Le Revard SNCF train station. Within easy reach of the town centre, you can find yourself swimming in the clear waters of Lac du Bourget, France’s largest and deepest lake or skiing or hiking on the mountains of Le Revard, France’s oldest ski station. We hope you will like the town as much as Queen Victoria, who came three times (and stayed a stone’s throw from 301 Le Carnot), the Romans who discovered thermal springs 131 BC and built Baths whose remains can still be seen, or the fast-growing number of French and foreign tourists who visit it at all seasons. We have put together this guide to help you get the most out of your time in this wonderful place, and be totally comfortable in the apartment during your stay. Wishing you a lovely stay!

Section 2: House rules

This section offers a general overview of the House rules. Please also refer to the information available on your booking platform. Do let us know if you have any question.

Check-in and check-out

Please conform to the times agreed upon booking and mentioned in your reservation. Early check-in and late check-out may be available upon request for a fee, subject to availability.

(No) Smoking policy

Smoking is strictly prohibited within the apartment, and in the common areas of the building. By exception, smokers can smoke on the balcony: please fold the window behind you and use the ashtray on the balcony A cleaning fee of €350 will be charged if evidence of smoking is found, and you may be asked to leave without a refund.

(No) Pets policy

Pets are not allowed in the apartment. A €350 fine will be charged for bringing a pet into the apartment, and you may be asked to leave without a refund.

Quiet hours

Please respect neighbours by keeping noise levels to a minimum between 10.00 pm and 8.00 am.
